sjoegele

a double pendulum simulator

‘Sjoegele’ ([ˈʃuɡələ]; Limburgish: swinging) simulatates a double pendulum. [Wikipedia article] You can use this for generating smooth chaotic control voltages.

With the θ₁ and θ₂ knobs and CV inputs, you can set the initial positions of the masses and you can set the length of the rods (in meters) and the gravititational acceleration with the corresponding knobs and inputs. The gravity knob has labels for the surface gravitiy of Pluto, the Moon, Mercury, Earth, Jupiter and the Sun. [Wikipedia article] These parameters are set when the ‘start’ button is hit, or the corresponding input receives a gate. You cannot change parameters on the fly.

The four outputs give the x and y positions of the masses; the x outputs as bipolar (–5 to 5 V) signals, the y outputs as unipolar (0 to 10 V) signals.

Sjoegele works with polyphony. The number of channels is determined by the maximum number of channels at the five inputs.
